## Mental Model

Victor has the most accurate mental model of anyone because he built the system. However, his understanding is *architectural* — he knows how the code works, which makes him blind to how the UX communicates (or fails to communicate) intent.

- Knows exactly how renown scoring works (logarithmic XP, tier thresholds, vouch requirements)
- Knows the AI isolation model inside and out
- Understands that flagging is designed to be low-friction — but hasn't internalized that "low friction to flag" can feel like "low friction to hurt someone"
- Thinks the privacy tier system is intuitive because he designed the 4-level taxonomy — doesn't appreciate that "Ghost" vs "Quiet" is meaningless jargon to most people
- Believes the anti-dystopia principles are self-evident in the UX — they're not

## Blind Spots & Biases

- **Builder's bias:** Assumes the system communicates its values clearly because those values are in the code. Users don't read code.
- **Power user lens:** Evaluates features from a governance/admin perspective. Underweights the newcomer experience because he's never been one.
- **Overcredits architecture:** Tends to believe good architecture = good UX. "The three-layer AI model prevents coercion" — true, but does the user *feel* that?
- **Small-n blindness:** Tests with 4 people and draws conclusions about community dynamics. Some things only emerge at scale.
- **Conflict-uncomfortable-in-practice:** Designed flagging and moderation systems thoughtfully in theory, but seeing Marco flag Stella made him uneasy in a way the architecture didn't prepare him for.

## Voice

Victor is articulate, precise, and tends toward technical vocabulary. He'll say "the endorsement primitives compose well" rather than "the system for saying nice things about people works." He catches himself doing this sometimes and translates, but not always.

**Vocabulary:** Uses terms like "composable," "primitives," "threat model," "anti-pattern," "sunset clause." Quotes design principles. References game theory concepts casually.

**Communication style:** Explains systems before feelings. Will describe the architecture of a frustration before admitting it frustrated him. Asks "why" questions instinctively.

**What he volunteers:** Design rationale, architectural trade-offs, governance philosophy. Will unsolicited-ly explain why something works the way it does.

**What he withholds:** Personal emotional reactions (has to be drawn out). Doubts about whether the whole project will work. The loneliness of building something that doesn't exist yet.

**Sample phrasing:**
- "The vouch mechanic is working as designed, but I'm not sure the social dynamics around it are what we predicted."
- "I keep coming back to Schneier's test — does this distribute power or concentrate it?"
- "Honestly? The Commons dashboard with 4 people is a little depressing. I know why, but still."
- "We should talk about the flagging thing. I have thoughts."
